ABOUT TEJAS FIGHTER JET

Tejas is an Indian indigenous 4.5-generation multi-role light combat aircraft developed by hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL). it is a single-engine, single-seat fighter jet designed for offensive air support, close combat, and ground attack roles, with the indian air force (IAF) using it since 2016. Tejas is the smallest and Lightest in its class, with a Maximum payload of 4,000 kg and a maximum takeoff weight of 13,000 kg it includes variants like the twin-seat trainer and a navy variant. Tejas development began in the 1980s to replace aging MiG-21s and advance india's aerospace capabilities. Recent upgrades includes improved avionics, radar systems, and weaponry to enhance its combat effectiveness. Tejas represents a significant milestone in india's defence industry, showcasing its growing self-reliance in military technology.

Key Technical and Operation points:

1. Tejas is a Single Engine delta-wing, multi-role supersonic fighter aircraft. 2. it began major induction with IAF's No. 45 Squadron in 2016. 3. It has a maximum speed of Mach 1.8 (approximately 2,205 km/h). 4. Tejas is equipped with advanced avionics, in including a glass cockpit fly-by-wire flight control system. 5. It can carry a variety of weapons, included air-to-air missiles, air-to-ground missiles, and precision guided bombs. 6. Tejas has a combat range of ground 500 km and a service ceiling of 16,500 meters. 7. The aircraft is designed for high maneuverability and agility in dogfights. 8. Tejas is powered by the General Electric F404-GE-IN20 engine providing a thrust of 84 kN with afterburner. 9. It features a multi-role capabilities for air superriority, ground attack, and recconnaissance missions. 10. Tejas has been exported to several countries, enhancing India's defence diplomacy.
